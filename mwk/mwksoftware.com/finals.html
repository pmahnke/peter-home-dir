Final Additions

Final additions calculations require just a few mouse clicks. That's all. The first click gets the sample chemistries from your spectrometer. The second click selects which sample chemistry you want to use. The third click starts the calculation and the results are displayed on the screen in the blink of an eye.

The furnace range / grade name that you are making is extracted from the spectrometer information along with the weight of your furnace / ladle. This information is used by the program to calculate your final additions. The appropriate melt stocks / alloys to be used for the final additions and the furnace range / grade specifications are set up automatically. The program can also automatically select the appropriate carbon equivalent, hardness (ASTM 255) requirement or any other additional constraint. No data entry by the operator is required. Just three mouse clicks and the calculation is done.

Here's how to get started:

You should download the demo program to your computer and get it set up and running on your computer. Then you can install the program to your spectrometer computer.

Take a look at the tutorial on the website. Basically you want to enter the elements you shoot for on your spectrometer on the Program Setup screen.

Then you want to enter the chemistries and costs of the materials you use for final additions. That might be high carbon ferro chrome but you would not include returns. The default inventory amount is NoLimit as the assumption is that you will always have the final additions materials available.

Now enter a Furnace Range, alloy specification, for an alloy you make. Be sure to enter a non zero upper limit for each element. Suppose you shoot for tungsten, W. If you have an upper limit of zero for W and your sample has 0.0001 you will end up with an invalid calculation as there is no way to dilute the W to zero.

Now create another melt stock that has the chemistry of one of your typical samples. You should make the chemistry OK, with in range, for all of the elements and just a bit low for one element. Select this melt stock as the chemistry of the current furnace.

Now you are ready for a final additions calculation. Enter the weight of the current furnace on the main screen and click on Continue. The report will show you what to add to correct the Current Furnace chemistry to the correct chemistry as specified in the Furnace Range, alloy specification.

You might do some more experimenting at this point but you are also ready to install the Final Additions Program on your spectrometer computer. Just install the program on the spectrometer computer the same way you installed it on your computer. Then copy all of the .dat files from the installation directory, generally c: \ MWK Software to the same directory on your spectrometer computer.
